# 2026-06-01 Abyssal Naming Committee

## Concept

A deep-sea taxonomy terminal for one politely unresolved golden orb. Each confident
classification lowers certainty and reveals additional inconvenient siblings.

The visual seed was the recent report about a mysterious golden orb recovered from
the ocean floor: [WIRED, 2026-05-23](https://www.wired.com/story/a-golden-orb-on-the-ocean-floor-came-from-a-mysterious-animal/).

## Test Plan

- Write the source-level contract before the page exists.
- Require a canvas abyss, golden specimen, live certainty and sibling readouts, pointer
  spotlight, bounded animation field, and gallery entry.
- Require a browser flow where classification lowers certainty, adds siblings, and
  pointer movement steers the research light.

## What Shipped

- A full-screen underwater scene with a canvas seafloor, debris field, sea grass,
  spotlight, vignette, glowing DOM orb, and expanding sibling specimens.
- A taxonomy button whose joke is the interaction itself: every classification makes
  the evidence less convenient.
- A mobile layout, `prefers-reduced-motion` handling, `MAX_MOTES = 84`, and a device
  pixel ratio cap of `2`.

## Feedback To Carry Forward

- Good, but the experiments are all feeling very similar in concept.
- Keep the canvas-atmosphere plus DOM-focal-object pattern available as a technique,
  but do not let it dictate the next premise.
- Reject the next idea if it repeats the same ambient scene, central prop, button press,
  escalating counter, and accumulating actor structure with a new coat of paint.

## Skills Or Patterns Learned

- Promote the canvas-atmosphere plus semantic-DOM-focal-object composition pattern in
  `memory/skills/canvas-spectacle-pages.md`.
- After validating queried nodes, create narrowed aliases before nested helpers capture
  them so Astro TypeScript checks remain clean.
