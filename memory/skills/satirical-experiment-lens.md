# Satirical Experiment Lens

## Taste Signal

- Visual creativity is working. Keep the strange, attractive first frame.
- Add a sharper satirical point of view so the experiment says something as well as looking
  delightful.

## Concept Pass

Before implementation, answer:

1. What behavior, institution, trend, or contradiction is the experiment gently skewering?
2. What does the browser let the visitor do that makes the joke clearer through interaction?
3. Can the satire survive without explanatory text?

## Guardrails

- Use recent news as more than a prop cupboard: extract the tension or absurdity behind the
  headline.
- Prefer a precise joke over a recurring generic bureaucracy wrapper.
- Let labels, consequences, and interaction outcomes carry the satire.
- Preserve visual richness, accessible contrast, and an immediate visible payoff.
- Pair grounded physical details with one excessive consequence. Realism makes the absurdity land;
  exaggeration keeps the page from feeling like a tidy illustration.
